UTHRAKALAMRITHA

By KALIDAS

 

(Any difference in results between Natal and Horary chart.)

 

Sloka 1: In the previous six chapters how to predict the results for a person taking his birth horoscope is explained. If no horoscope is available the results can be predicted by taking the time of query. The results obtained by scrutinising the birth chart or the Horary chart erected for the time of query will be the same. They will not differ. If at the time of query it is cloudy and it is not possible to ascertain the exact time which would have passed from sunrise then by putting further questions to the querist, his horoscope can be erected.

 

(What the Consultant and Astrologer should do.)

 

Sloka 2: The consultant is advised to take fruits and fees to the astrologer. If the querist is asked to give any number between one and 108, he should give any one number. Then the astrologer takes the number for further calculation and prediction whether the result is favourable or not. The astrologer explains the past and future after he prays to his diety.

 

(How to erect the horoscope of a person who has lost it)

 

Sloka 3: Divide by 9 the number given by the consultant. Note down the quotient and the remainder. The former denotes how many rasis (signs) from Aries (Mesha) has to be counted and the latter indicates the exact Navamsa to be taken in the next sign. Work out the position of the planet and note down the Bhavas for this horoscope; one has to predict the results whether advantageous or not. One should consider the strength of the planets and also to which matters each planet is the Chief Governor.

 

Sloka 4: Calculate the number of flowers and the units of money given to the astrologer by the consultant. He is to multiply this figure by the number given by the consultant, and then divide by 12. Note down the remainder. Jupiter will be deposited in the horoscope of the consultant in that sign which is obtained by counting from Aries with the remainder. Jupiter at the time of consultation will be in a sign. The astrologer has to look at the consultant and Guess his age and calculate how many times Jupiter would have completed the cycle of the zodiac.

 

Note how far Jupiter has passed in the sign, from the commencement of that sign at the consultation. So much will be the number of months and days which would have passed from the previous birthday of the consultant. Thus by noting the number of cycles made by the Jupiter and also the proportionate movement of Jupiter in the sign at the time of consultation you calculate the time passed after his birth. Or the sum is to be deducted from the time of the query. The result is to be taken as the exact time of the birth of the consultant.

 

Sloka 5: All the above calculations ars in Solar System, i.e., the month, the day, the nazhikhai (ghatis!). (I Ghati = 24 minutes = 1 Nazhikhai). Having obtained the birth time one can calculate and erect the complete horoscope, of the consultant who would have lost his.

 

Sloka 6: The consultant has given a number; multiply it by 2 and then divide the product by three. The question will be about a living object if the remainder is one. If the remainder is two, it will be about some mineral; if the remainder is three or zero, then the question would be about a plant. If at the time of question the sign Aries is the Lagna it represents two- legged bipeds. If the lagna falls in Rishaba it represents four- legged quadrupeds. If the Ascendant be Gemini it denotes pregnancy. Legal dispute will be the matter if Cancer rises, Question will be about the Government if Leo is the lagna.

 

Sloka 7: If the Ascendant falls in Virgo, the question will be about the secret activities with a lady other than his own wife. If Libra is the ascendant the question relates to one's business. It will be about one's fear from enemies, if the ascendant is Scorpio. Sagittarius, as ascendant indicates the loss of money. Capricorn denotes any undesirable action of the consultant. Aquarius being the ascendant indicates one's rank position, prestige, house or dwelling. Sages say that one should note whether the Rasi or Navamsa is strong. Select the stronger of the two. They follow the above rules to declare the result.

 

Sloka 8: (In this sloka the author gives the various matters indicated by each planet.)

 

(a) If Sun is deposited either in Rasi or Navamsa in his own sign, at the time of the consultation, then the matter relates to the king or the country.

(b) If in his own Rasi or Navamsa whichever is stronger, Moon is situated, then it indicates lakes, etc.

(c) Mars in his own Rasi or Navamsa denotes fire, danger, accident or dispute.

(d) Mercury similarly placed indicates business or cultivation.

(e) Jupiter shows that the question would be about the consultant's benefactors or high officials or the ruler etc.

(f) Venus indicates about one's smooth run of life.

(g) Saturn portrays that the queries relate to a person or a property.

 

Sloka 9: (Strength of the planets and the extent of the result). Exalted planets give double the effect; planets in debilitation produce no effect at all; planets in their friend's quarters give only 50% of the result. If a planet is in a neutral house the effect is very moderate. Suppose there are two planets in exaltation or they are in their own signs, then select the stronger of the two and note to which matter the stronger planet is the chief governor; also consider the house occupied by it.

 

Sloka 10: (Time of event). The matter will be fulfilled immediately if the significator be exalted. If it is in his own sign, in a few days, it will be realised. If it is in friend's quarters, it takes a few months. If it is neither exalted nor in its own or friendly sign one can realise only after some years; if a planet be in conjunction with or aspected by an evil planet, then the consultant will be disappointed: there will be only failure. If the lord of the ascendant is deposited in the house which is to be judged for that query or if the lord of the ascendant is aspected by strong benefics the native will have success, at once.

 

Sloka 11: (Direction allotted to each sign): The zodiac is a circle of 360 degrees, and there are 12 signs of 30 degrees each also. The zodiac is divided into 108 Navamsas, these are all fixed. Kalidas explains how each sign indicates a particular direction. This does not agree with other authors

 

Sloka 12 (Aruda Lagna): Suppose a person with flowers and fruits approaches an astrologer and is in a place to put a query, then one has to take the sign indicated by the direction in which he is standing. That sign is called Aruda Lagna at the time of query. An Astrologer should consider the owner of the Aruda Lagna and that particular house which indicates the nature of the query. He should include, while judging the planets conjoined or aspected by them. The astrologer has to predict such questions whether it may be t desirable or not,

 

Slokas 13, 14 and 15: Note down the gatikas which have passed from the time of sunrise to the time of consultation. Multiply the number of gatikas by nine. Then divide by 20. The quotient indicates the position of Moon at that time. This Moon is called Sukshma Moon. But the position of Moon for the moment of consultation according to ephemeris is called Sthula Moon. The Navamsas of both the Sthula and Sukshma Moon is to be found out. If the Sthula Moon receives aspect from the Sukshma Moon, Jupiter and Mercury, then the consultant wants to know about an animal. But if Mars, Rahu and Saturn aspect the Sthula Moon, the question would be about a mineral. If Sun and Venus aspects the matter will refer to vegetable kingdom. Jupiter, Venus and Rahu aspecting the Sthula Moon indicates many matters; if the Sthula Moon occupies Gemini, Virgo or Libra Navamsa, the question is about two-legged bipeds. If the Sthula Moon is in a watery sign which indicates multi-legged creatures. If the Sthula Moon occupies Sagittarius and Aquarius Navamsa, the question is about some of God without legs. If the Sthula Moon is in Aries, Taurus or Capricorn the matter indicated is about four legged (quadrupeds) one is to know in which portion of a rasi a planet is situated. Sign is to be divided into three equal parts and the first part is called youth, the second Yauvana and the third Vridha. These are the three conditions of a planet situated in a sign.

 

Sloka 16: Signs from Aries are movable, fixed and common. The movable signs Aries, Cancer, Libra and Capricorn are called Garba. The fixed ones are called devara and the common ones are called Bahya. Garba and Devara indicate an object held in the right hand whereas Bahya shows that in left hand.

 

Sloka 17: Thus one should guess a thing kept close in the hand whether it is short or long, is to be judged from the length of the rasi. (Signs are classified as short ascension and long ascension). The shape, the colour of the material is to be judged for the Navamsa sign. The nature of the cloud reveals whether the object is beneficial or not. The place is to be judged from the sign whether it is on land or sky or water or mountains. Also the sign indicates whether it is wicked or uncontrolled for tamed or polite.

 

Sloka 18 (Time of accomplishment): The consultant gives a number within 108; divide by 5. The quotient is to be added to the number given. Divide the same by 60. In so many Ghatikas the matter will be fulfilled. Divide by 30 you get so many thithis. Divide the sum by 7 it indicates so many days after consultation. Divide by 12 this shows the number of months; divide by 60 the remainder indicates the number of years. This method is to be followed for all questions.

 

Sloka 19: If the lord of the house which is to be judged for a particular question and the lord of the lagna are benefic by nature and also occupy good house mutually aspecting each other the matter will be fulfilled satisfactorily.
