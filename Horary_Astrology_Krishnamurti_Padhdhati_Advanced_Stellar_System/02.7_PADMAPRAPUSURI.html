PADMAPRAPUSURI

By Shri PADMAPRAPUSURI

 

He prays to Lord Ganesh and describes the fundamentals like the names of 12 signs; planets; the exaltation, debilitation; etc.

 

According to him, morning is indicated by Jupiter and Mercury. Evening is indicated by Moon and Venus. The day, by Sun and Mars whereas Rahu and Saturn denote the time of sunset.

 

Jupiter and Venus denote two-legged ones; Sun and Mars represent four-legged one: Saturn and Mercury denote birds: Moon and Rahu, the reptiles. Moon indicates large ones Venus flat one; Sun and Mars square: Mercury and Jupiter round; Rahu and Saturn tall. (Hindu authors give different versions.)

 

Mars indicates red colour; Mercury green; Jupiter golden; Moon handsome: Sun brilliant red: Venus white: Saturn and Rahu, black.

 

Mercury indicates child; Mars young man; Moon and Venus adults; Sun, Jupiter, Saturn and Rahu indicates aged people.

 

Then, he describes what the twelve houses indicate.

 

He also gives the method, by which one has to measure out the shadow and calculate the time of query. This is not at all applicable to different latitudes, since in many latitudes, shadow is so long, that it cannot be measured at all. And this method may be tried by people living close to the Equator. Application of this rule is limited. Only day time is to be preferred —— Cloudy days are out of question. When time-piece is available, there is no use for this method. Then, he gives the method by which one is to find out whether the lagna is strong, and also the bhava to be judged, is strong. It is general, and it can never come and it will mostly fail. Then, he deals with eclipse planet. He claims that when lord of lagna aspects the lagna which is to be judged, then the promotion of the bhava of the success is to be predicted. This is also not correct. He gives his opinion that if lord of lagna or lord of 11 is situated in the 11th house, one will gain. Stellar Astrology disproves this rule. Again, his opinion about the houses 6, 8 and 12 is not correct.

 

In Stanza 87, he discusses whether one will have safe deli- very or not. He says that if lord of 5 is deposited or is aspecting the 5th, then the delivery is not easy: if an evil planet is in 5, or aspects 5, one is to predict miscarriage. But, in practice, it was found that lord 5 in 5, deposited in the constellation of lord of 8 in 8, has caused cessarian operation. Constellation theory alone, will prove to be successful. He says that twin birth is to be predicted, if 8 planets are deposited in 4 houses, 2 in each. Such a combination will be very rare. (So, research cannot be successfully done in one's lifetime.) He says that the age of the intra-uterine life of the Foetus is, as many number of months as the number of houses, where Venus is situated from the lagna. Thus, he goes on dealing in his book.

 

My opinion is that the whole thing is general and is not at all helpful to exactly pin-point the nature of the event and the time of the event.

 

As regards disease, he says Vayu: Pitha; sleshma like that. All out of date-Anyhow, I have to say that it is not useful for modern times.
