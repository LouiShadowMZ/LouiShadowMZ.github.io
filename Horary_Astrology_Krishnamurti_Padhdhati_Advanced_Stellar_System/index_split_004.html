2. The following extract is taken from the book "Modern Text-book of Astrology" by MARGARET E HONE (page 272).

 

If the question comes to an astrologer's mind, he casts the chart for the moment of his thought, using the latitude and longitude of the place where he is. If a person comes to an astrologer and puts his request, the time of the request is taken.

 

Diversity of opinion exists as to the procedure to follow when there is delay through the post. Some astrologers say that the time of opening the letter should be taken, others stating that the querent should give the time when the idea came to birth in his own mind. This seems the more reasonable way.

 

The chart having been cast, the ruler of the sign on the first cusp is reckoned to represent the querent, and the ruler of the sign on the cusp of the house connected with the affairs of the question is taken as its significator. Judgment depends mainly on the aspect formed between these two, but many minor rules to be followed have bearing on this.

 

The practice of Horary Astrology was very popular in the Middle ages, and its best known exponent was William Lilly (1602-81). His Life and Works may be read, also a book of his aphorisms and maps, edited by the astrologer Zadikiel (Commander R. J. Morrison). He states that every rule must be meticulously followed, but unfortunately he himself begins the book with Tables of Houses calculated for the Placidean System of house division and follows by Lilly's maps, all calculated according to the system of Regiomontanus.

 

As many astrological students have accepted the Placidean system because its Tables are the only one easily accessible, and as few realise that Lilly died before this system was introduced into England, it may be wondered why Zadikiel did not mention this.

 

In Horary work, aspects to house cusps are held to be as important as those to planets. This presented no problem to most astrologers of the last few generations, since they accepted the Placidean system and said their maps" worked".

HOW WESTERNERS JUDGE A HORARY CHART

Westerners do not ask for a number, but they erect a horoscope for the moment of judgment and they read out results according to the aspects which are being formed (applying) and also the aspects which are separating.

 

Aspects: They do not include minor aspects as they consider that they are not of any significance. But they take for judgment only the following aspects. These aspects may be either between a planet and a cusp of a house, or mutual aspects between two planets. The aspects which are included are: the conjunction; the semi-sextile; semi-square; sextile; square: trine: sesquiquadrate and opposition. When two planets have the same geocentric latitude, it is said to be in conjunction. This is considered to be good if beneficial planets conjoin. But if malefic planets conjoin together, it is considered to be evil. semi-sextile aspect is moderate in strength and it is advantageous. The longitudinal distance will be 30. The Semisguare aspect is ever evil whether it is among the benefics or among the malefics. It is of 45°. The sextile aspect at 60° is considered to be more favourable than semi-sextile. But much less than the trine. The sextile is of 60°. The square aspect of 90° is very bad, and very evil. The aspect may be among two benefics or among two malefics. Whatever it is, this aspect is very detrimental. The trine aspect of 120° is very strong in offering most desirable results. Whether the aspect is among the benefics or among the malefics, or between benefic and malefic, the sesquisquarate aspect is of 135 and it is an unlucky one. The opposition of 180° is considered to be malefic if either of the two planets happens to be a malefic. But if both of them: happen to be benefics then, even though aspect is called opposition, yet the results will be agreeable.

 

Orbs: In a horoscope (whether natal or horary) the planets will not be always in the exact number of degrees as 30, 45, 60 and so on. It is to be remembered that every planet has got an influence round its body. Uranus has 7°, Saturn 9°, Jupiter 9°, Sun 15, Mars 7°, Venus 7°, Mercury 7° and Moon 15°.

 

If one wants to know whether the planets are forming an aspect, taking into consideration the orbs, then one has to add the orbs of both the planets and divide by 2. So take half of the sum. If the aspect which is to be formed is within the actual distance plus half of the sum (half the orbs of the two planets) then they are said to be forming the aspect. The results also should be given as though they are having the aspect (applying aspect).

When will the event happen?

 

They confess that it is very difficult to judge, accurately. They limit the time by taking either the house or the sign or by aspect. To find out the number of days, weeks, months and years, they consider the actual distance between the planet and the cusp of the house or among two planets and depending upon the number of degrees in between their aspect, they predict the days, weeks, the months and years. Also, they include in their prediction, in which house the planets are deposited. If the houses are succedent ones, the result will be in so many months or years according to the sign, being movable, common or fixed. But cadent houses give in months in movable signs; years in common ones and far beyond in fixed sign. South latitude increases the duration, whereas north latitude reduces.

 

They also take the transit into consideration and from the transiting position, they offer the prediction how a matter will progress.

 

Distance: To find out the distance, they select the planet which signifies the matter. If it is without latitude, the distance is short. But if it is in North Latitude, it will be in furlongs whereas if it is in South Latitude, it will be in miles. They calculate the number of miles taking the number of degrees and minutes between the planet and the significator and according to the distance in degrees and minutes, they mention the same number of miles.

 

Aries, Cancer, Libra and Capricorn show two miles for every degree. Taurus, Leo, Scorpio and Aquarius for every degree indicates two furlongs, whereas Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius and Pisces denote four furlongs for every degree. Therefore, from the number of degrees and minutes, we have to multiply and give out the answer. Their principle of judgment depends only on the aspects and the longitudinal distance
