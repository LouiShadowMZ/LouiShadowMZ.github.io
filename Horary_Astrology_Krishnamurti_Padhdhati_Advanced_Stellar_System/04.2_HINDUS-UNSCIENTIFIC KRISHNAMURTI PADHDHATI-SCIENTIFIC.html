HINDUS-UNSCIENTIFIC KRISHNAMURTI PADHDHATI-SCIENTIFIC

Horary Astrology followed by the Hindus is not scientific. There are two methods:

(1) Ask for a number within 108 and divide by 12; take the remainder and counting from Aries depending on the reminder, we fix a sign as lagna. We take the whole sign as lagna bhava, the next sign is second bhava and so on. Hence the longitude of each bhava will be 30°. Only when the number given happens to be 6 or 18 or 30 or 42 or 54 or 66 or 78 or 90 or 102, ascendant Virgo will rise for latitudes 14° to 18° N, the longitude of each house will be more or less the same, each being 30°. Otherwise, the longitude of each house must be different.

(2) We ask for number within 108 and divide it by 9 count from Aries, as many signs as the quotient is, then the remainder indicates the Navamsa in the next sign. So the number given by the consultant gives the navamsa, the longitude of which 3°20′. The house division is not suggested by the author in Uthrakalamridha. So, one is to take, naturally, 30°, 30° from that Navamsa or 15° behind and 15° in advance according to the Hindu system of house division. The author has not advocated as to which method is to be followed. But if we follow cusp to cusp, just like Westerners, then also we have to take 30°, as the longitude of each house. The longitude of each house will be 30° only when the number given is 47, and that also, will be nearing correctness only for 200 years to come as from 1st January, 1970, Ayanamsa is 23° 20′; one may refer to Raphael's Tables of Houses. Then one can find that, for latitudes 14° to 18° N when Libra 0 rises in the East, the other cusps are all in 0 in the next, next sign. (The sidereal time will be 6 hrs. 0 mts. 0 secs.) Therefore only for the latitudes 14 to 18° N, when according to Sayana 0 Libra rises in the East, the houses will be of 30. Otherwise, the longitude of each must vary.

But in Krishnamurti Padhdhati, the number indicates the sign, the star and the sub. And the sub commences at a particular position. This is taken as the ascendant and according to the latitude of the locality, where the horoscope is judged, taking into account the Ayanamsa on the day of judgment, the cusps of the other houses are calculated. Therefore, Krishnamurti Padhdhati is alone scientific, whereas the Hindu system is, in fact, unscientific.

Regarding Westerners they take the time of judgment; they crect the horoscope, for that time taking into account, the latitude of the place of judgment. So, it is very scientific.

But the defect in that system is that certain questions can never be answered using the horoscope erected for a particular moment of judgment. Suppose two people come and ask, within an interval of a few minutes. The horoscope, the aspects between the planets and the cusps of the houses, the mutual aspects among the planets themselves, will remain more or less the same, and therefore one has to predict the same result for both of them. (Time of event and the nature of the result). Supposing A and B stand for election in the same locality and both of them visit the house of an astrologer at an interval, of 5 minutes, the astrologer has to necessarily offer the same prediction to both of them.

But if the astrologer were to ask for a number and accordingly he erects the horoscope, certainly

(a) the position of the ascendant and the other cusps of the houses will vary, even though the planets may be in the same sign, same star, and same sub:

(b) the houses occupied by the planets will vary:

(c) the significators will vary and hence one can correctly predict the diametrically opposite results.

Therefore, Krishnamurti Padhdhati-Horary Astrology- is most scientific and most useful for any question, and this gives precisely the correct answer and also the accurate date of fruition.
