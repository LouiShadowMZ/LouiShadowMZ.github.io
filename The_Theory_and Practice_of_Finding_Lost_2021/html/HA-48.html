Appendix VI

A LOST AND FOUND HORARY
BY A MASTER PRACTITIONER

Astrologer Gary Price, who has been practicing horary for decades, kindly gave me permission to reproduce one of his charts, which appeared in the first edition of Price’s The Horary Practioner back in 1989. His analysis is brief and to the point and serves as an excellent teaching example for students of the art. Currently Gary is writing a book on horary with an emphasis on predicting the outcome of games and competitions. The following is Gary’s horary delineation verbatim.

After searching over two hours for a receipt for an item my wife and I were buying, I decided to draw a horary chart and consulted Lilly to see what he had to say about hidden or mislaid things [chart 49]. Here is what I found.

If you find the 2nd house ruler in any angle, the missing item is within the querent’s house (CA 202). If the Moon is with the 1st house ruler in the same quarter of heaven and no more than one sign separates them, the lost item is in the home of the person who lost it (CA 320).

Jupiter rules the 2nd house and is angular [in the 7th house] in Gemini, a sign representing west by south (CA 204). Jupiter rules the 4th house as well as the 2nd, making him doubly important because the 4th house governs misplaced things.

The Moon is in Pisces, a northwest sign. Jupiter is angular in the western hemisphere, so I concluded that mislaid receipt was west. Mercury, the natural ruler of papers, disposes Jupiter and occupies Libra, a western sign. The majority of significators in air and fire signs led me to conclude the object was aboveground; fiery signs show near a wall or partition (CA 204).

Chart 49: Gary Price: Where Is the Missing Receipt?
25 September 1988, 10:40 a.m. GMT (11:40 a.m. BST), London. Sun day, Saturn hour.
Regiomontanus houses. Dwad of Ascendant is at 25o 24' Cancer.

If you find the Moon applying to the 1st house ruler, or to the 12th house ruler from the 1st, or to the Moon’s dispositor, the missing thing will be found again (CA 319). The Moon, although void of course [by the modern definition], still performs in Pisces and applies, after changing signs, to Mars, who rules both the 1st and 12th (CA 89). If the Moon’s dispositor is in a human sign, it is in a place frequented by people (CA 320). Jupiter, which disposes the Moon, is in Gemini, a human sign. In addition, Jupiter is Retrograde, showing a return.

The 1st house ruler Mars last separated from Saturn (when Mars was in Pisces). That means the thing was lost through forgetfulness (CA 321). I pondered this for a clue as to what I was doing when I misplaced it. The only activity I could remember was astrology, and the receipt could be among my books, but I have so many. The west wall has many books on its shelves. Rather than search through them all, I decided to approach the chart from a different angle. There are two fireplaces along the west wall, one a gas fire in the living room, the other a radiator in the bedroom. I decided to check the books next to the radiator in the bedroom, as I had placed some astrology books there after studying. Among the books was a small, multi-colored notepad with alternating red, white, yellow, and blue pages. Tucked away in the red pages was the receipt. The books were on a stack of other books, high up off the floor. Mars rules the color red, and Mercury rules multi-colored things. Mars rules the 12th of bedrooms and the 5th of pleasure. Most of my astrology reading takes place in the bedroom. (Price 1989, 22)

A Note on the 12th Part in Price’s “Missing Receipt” Horary

Although Gary Price did not use 12th parts in his delineation, let’s consider what the dwad of the Ascendant might have indicated to the astrologer. The Ascendant at 22o 07' Scorpio has its 12th part at 25o 24' Cancer in the 8th Regiomontanus house, which can signify things that are lost, dead, “lying obscurely,” or concealed from view (CA 470). Cancer is the 9th Whole Sign from the Ascendant, so the querent may have a matter related to books or higher learning on his mind. There are no visible planets in Cancer, which makes the placement of Cancer’s ruler, the Moon, highly significant. The Moon lies in Pisces in the 4th house of home, family, and mislaid things. The Moon’s most recent aspect to a visible planet was a square to Saturn in Sagittarius in the 2nd Regiomontanus house. The Moon’s separation from Saturn indicates concern about having forgotten where he put something (CA 321), and the 2nd house refers to purchases and movable goods. If we string together the keywords, we can guess that the following issues were on the querent’s mind when he asked the question:

• 8th house—things “lying obscurely”

  • 9th house—books, higher learning
  • 4th house—home, family, things mislaid
  • 2nd house—purchases and movable goods
  • Moon most recently separating from Saturn—something misplaced due to carelessness, distraction, or forgetfulness
