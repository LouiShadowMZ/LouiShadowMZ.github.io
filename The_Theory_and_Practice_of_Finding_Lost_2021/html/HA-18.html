Chapter 18

THE SIGNS

Finding lost objects with horary involves describing the location where they are likely to have been misplaced or to be currently located. This chapter presents quotations from Lilly on this topic, followed by my own elaborations of Lilly’s ideas.

Locations and the Elementary Nature of the Signs

Lilly writes:

The nature of the Signs showed me also the quality of the place in the house, viz.

• Airy Signs, above ground;

• Fiery Signs, near a Wall or Partition;

• Earthly Signs, on the Floor;

• Watery Signs, near a moist place in the room, &c. (CA 204)

Air Signs (Yang)

Above ground, in upper rooms, an attic, an open space, on an upper floor of the house, high up from the floor or ground, on a shelf, at or near the top of a tall piece of furniture; near a window, windowsill, or where air can flow; hanging in the air, like laundry on a clothesline (we generally avoid airing our dirty laundry in public). Both Fire and Air signs indicate locations higher up in a room; and, like Fire signs, the Air signs can also signify that an item is hanging from a hook or fastener, or possibly from a doorknob. Air circulates as wind above the earth.

Fire Signs (Yang)

Near a wall or partition, perhaps hanging from a hook or fastener on the wall, or possibly from a doorknob; near a chimney, stove, radiator, or other heat source; near a light or lamp, at mid-height or waist level (heat rises); with tools or near objects made of iron or steel (ruled by fiery Mars), in a place related to combustion or the use of heat or fire, in a garage or in a car. In one case in my files, the significator in a Fire sign reflected the fact that the querent left the missing item in a garden shed (with rakes and shovels made of iron). Heat rises, and Fire signs generally refer to places aboveground. In another case, the 2nd and 4th house significators (the missing possession and its location, respectively) were both in Fire signs, and the missing keys were found in a purse hanging from a doorknob between two rooms.

Earth Signs (Yin)

Earth signs suggest locations on the floor, near or at ground level, in the ground, beneath the floor or pavement, in a basement, etc. Because earth forms the ground that we walk on, Earth signs can sometimes signify places or things that are dusty, dirty, dark, solid, “soiled,” well trodden, or made of earth or clay. Outdoors, the Earth signs can refer to parks and gardens, or to locations that are muddy or near a bridge. Things in the earth are often covered, hidden, concealed, or contained within an enclosed space.

Water Signs (Yin)

Near a moist or damp place, near water, water fixtures, or liquids of any kind; “in the buttery, dairy, or wash house” (CA 203); in a comfortable location. Water descends to the bottom of its container, and Water signs often refer to places that are low down, on the floor, at or beneath ground level, or below a main room. If contained within something, the item may be in the lower portion or below a main compartment of the container, as in the bottom of a bag or pocketbook, in a lower drawer, on a lower shelf, etc. Water is a feminine yin element, and a useful mnemonic for remembering locations indicated by Water signs might be an image of a mother carrying an unborn child in amniotic fluid in her womb.

Signs: Leading with Their Forehead or Their Back Side

Indian astrology takes note of whether the constellation of stars associated with each sign rises with its head or its back. The Shirshodaya constellations depicting the Twins, the Lion, the Virgin, the Scales, the Scorpion, and the Water-Bearer rise headfirst over the eastern horizon, followed by the rest of their bodies. This straightforward approach to rising is considered auspicious and helps horary matters to proceed smoothly and directly.

On the other hand, the Prishtodaya star groups depicting the Ram, the Bull, the Crab, the Man-Horse Archer, and the Sea-Goat (Crocodile) are said to rise with their rear parts as they traverse the eastern horizon. This “backward” approach to rising is considered somewhat inauspicious and may indicate impediments, delays, or carelessness related to the question and its resolution. Only the two Fishes, swimming in opposite directions, rise sideways (neither headfirst nor rear first), indicating a mixed influence.

I have not tested this concept extensively, but it may provide clues to finding lost objects or explaining how they were misplaced. For example, if the significator of a missing object falls in a sign whose hindquarters are first to rise, then the item might be found by retracing one’s steps (going back), or it may quite literally be found in a place associated with the rear of people or things, such as a rear pocket, a backpack, a jacket worn on one’s back, the back seat of a car, or the cushions of a couch that one sits on. In addition, the querent may have misplaced the item by not having followed their usual, standard, or expected manner of storing it. In one case in my files, Sagittarius rises rear-end first, and the querent, in a rush, had placed some important papers out of view on top of a tall cabinet with the intention of later filing them where he normally keeps his documents. He then forgot that he had put the papers high up and out of view, but fortunately the horary chart enabled him to locate the missing item.

Where Is My Silk Scarf?

My wife is forever misplacing things. On the evening of 15 December 2017, we had dinner at a Japanese restaurant with some friends. When we arrived home, my wife noticed that the silk scarf she had on at the restaurant was missing. At 8:30 p.m. EST, she asked me to help her find the scarf. I quickly cast a horary chart (chart 17).

Chart 17: Where Is My Silk Scarf?
15 December 2017, 8:30 p.m. EST, 41N17, 73W02. Venus day, Mercury hour.
Regiomontanus houses. Dwad of Ascendant is at 16o 48' Aquarius.

It is a Venus day during a Mercury hour. The Ascendant-ruler Sun and the lords of the day and hour (Venus and Mercury) are all conjunct in Sagittarius in the 5th house. The chart appears radical.

My wife is shown by the Leo Ascendant. Her ruler is the Sun, and her co-ruler is the Moon.

The missing scarf is signified by Mercury, ruler of the Virgo 2nd house of movable goods. Mercury is retrograde, suggesting that the scarf will return to her. The Moon can also signify missing items, as can the dispositor of the Part of Fortune, which is also Mercury in this chart. Venus is a natural signifier of women’s clothing.

All the signifiers of the scarf lie in Sagittarius, the 5th Whole Sign house; and all but the Moon lie in the 5th Regiomontanus house. Because the 5th house signifies fun, good times with friends, enjoyable meals, and restaurants, I suggested that she call the restaurant.

In addition, the location of missing items is often revealed by studying the ruler of the 4th house (where treasure is hidden), which in this chart is Mars in Scorpio. Mars conjoins the 4th house cusp (the subterranean house), and Scorpio (a Water sign) implies a location that is lower down and may be dark or dirty (which suggested to me that the scarf may be on the floor beneath the table at the restaurant).

Mars is just leaving the 3rd house (local travel, communications, phone calls) and entering the 4th (the querent’s home, the location of buried treasure), suggesting that a phone call might bring her scarf home. Mars at the cusp of the 4th is an indication that the item may have fallen down and be behind or beneath something that obscures its view. Recall Lilly’s comment about significators at boundaries:

If your Significator be going out of one Sign and entering another, the thing is behind something or other, or is carelessly fallen down betwixt two rooms, or near the Threshold, or joining together of two rooms, and is higher or lower in the place, according to the nature of the Sign, &c. (CA 203)

I told my wife that her scarf was probably on the floor under the table where we ate at the restaurant.

Within five minutes of her asking the question, she called the restaurant and they found her scarf on the floor under the table and against the wall where she had eaten, just as the chart indicated. Fire signs indicate a location near a wall or partition. Apparently the significator of the 2nd house cusp (Mercury) in Sagittarius meant that the scarf was against a wall, and the significator of the 4th house (Mars) in Scorpio indicated that it was on the floor.

Her rapid recovery of the scarf was probably shown by Mercury retrograde applying to a trine to the Ascendant in less than one degree. In addition, the Moon’s North Node conjunct the Ascendant and the Part of Fortune conjunct the 2nd house cusp are indications of prompt recovery.

Retrograde Significators and the Void-of-Course Moon

In the previous example of my wife’s missing scarf, the 2nd house ruler (Mercury) is retrograde, that is, in apparent backward motion in the sky, so that it is moving backward toward its point of departure. When the significator of a missing item is retrograde, the item will usually be found, unless there are strong indications to the contrary in the chart.

In missing object horaries, the void-of-course Moon often indicates that the item will be found. To the best of my recollection, it was one of my horary teachers, Joan McEvers, who provided me with the reasoning for this interpretation. The Moon is a general signifier of fugitives and things that wander, that is, lost or missing possessions. When the Moon is void of course, it is unable to connect with the other planets in the chart. Thus, the void Moon (a symbol of things that have gone astray) can’t do a good job of staying lost and the item is more easily found. Owing to its void state, the Moon’s desire to wander and remain missing is rendered futile and ineffective.

Kevin’s Keys

The following horary illustrates the above principles. Kevin kindly gave me permission to discuss his horary question, which he posted on 12 November 2019 in the Facebook Horary Astrology Group. Kevin “lost” his car keys sometime between Sunday, 10 November 2019, and Tuesday, 12 November 2019, and he asked the horary question “Where are the car keys?” on 12 November 2019 at 16:15:44 p.m. EST in Boca Raton, Florida (Chart 18). Instead of using Lilly’s preferred Regiomontanus houses, Kevin cast the chart with Campanus houses, which produce similar house cusps. Placidus houses would have placed different signs on some of the cusps. When analyzing another astrologer’s horary chart, I stick to the house system originally used, in this case Campanus houses.

The question was asked on a Mars day (Tuesday) during a Mercury hour. Because the lord of the hour, Mercury, is not of the same triplicity or nature as the lord of the Ascendant, Mars, Lilly would advise proceeding with caution because the question may not be “radical,” or “fit to be judged.” On the other hand, Mercury is a natural signifier of keys, and it is symbolically appropriate that Mercury rules the 2nd house of movable possessions in the house system chosen by this querent. In addition, Mars rules iron and is symbolically connected to mechanics and combustion engines. Thus, the chart appears to be radical and fit to be judged because it adequately describes the situation.

Chart 18: Kevin: Where Are the Car Keys?
12 November 2019, 16:15:44 p.m. EST, Boca Raton, FL. Mars day, Mercury hour.
Campanus houses. Dwad of Ascendant is at 5o 36' Pisces. Late degree rising.

Kevin goes on to tell us that on Sunday, he went with his family to a Buddhist center and then to the beach and finally to visit some friends, who later informed him that he did not leave the keys at their house. On Monday, he didn’t go anywhere. Looking at the chart, we see that Aries rises, making Mars the planet that signifies Kevin. Mars has just completed a sextile to Jupiter in Sagittarius, and Jupiter rules the Sagittarius 9th house (religious sites and expansive outdoor locations) and the Pisces 12th house (retreat centers and the ocean). Again, the symbolism fits the events.

With Gemini on the 2nd Campanus cusp, Mercury rules the keys. Mercury is also a natural signifier of keys. Had Kevin used Placidus or a Whole Sign house system, Taurus would have been on the 2nd house cusp and we would take Venus to rule the keys. Nonetheless, Kevin chose to use the Campanus system, and because horary is a divinatory system, the universe prompted him to ask the question at the appropriate moment to provide an answer. As Morinus pointed out in book 18 of his Astrologia Gallica, it is useful to consider the Whole Sign places in addition to the quadrant houses when delineating charts.

What planet(s) signify the missing keys in this chart? First and most important, Mercury rules the keys because of Gemini on the 2nd Campanus cusp and also because Mercury is a natural signifier of keys. Second, the Moon co-rules the missing keys because the Moon is a signifier of things that stray or wander. Third, the Part of Fortune and its dispositor can be a symbol for material goods. Lastly, Venus has a signification of personal possessions as ruler of the 2nd Whole Sign from the Ascendant.

Note that Mercury is retrograde, meaning that it is in apparent backward motion in the heavens and is headed back toward its point of departure, which was with the querent. The retrograde status of the planet signifying a missing object often means that the item will return. In addition, the Moon is void of course, meaning that it will not be within orb of aspecting any planet before leaving its sign. Some of the older texts call this state “being in an aspect vacuum.” The Moon is kind of floating in space, unable to connect to any other visible planet and just waiting for the querent to come and find her. She is unable to wander away or get lost successfully because of her void-of-course status. Both of these testimonies suggest that the missing keys will come back to Kevin.

Where are the keys? The primary significator, Mercury, is in Scorpio in the 7th, which is an angular house. Angular houses refer to a place nearby and often frequented by the querent, such as the querent’s home, car, or office. Specifically, however, the 7th house refers to one’s spouse or partner, so the keys are likely to be with his spouse or in a location that she frequents. Kevin’s wife is triply emphasized in this horary because 2nd ruler Mercury lies in the 7th, as does the Part of Fortune. In addition, the dispositor of the Part of Fortune in Scorpio is Mars, which closely conjoins the 7th house cusp. This triple emphasis strongly suggests that the keys are with his wife or in a place that she frequents. If we allow Venus, as ruler of the 2nd Whole Sign, to signify the keys, then Venus in Sagittarius lies in the second quadrant house of the 7th house wife, which is also the third Whole Sign house of the 7th house partner. Thus, the missing keys could be with the wife’s possessions (8th house as 2nd of 7th) or with something connected with her short-distance travels (9th house as 3rd of 7th).

Why haven’t the keys been found yet? The main signifier of the keys, Mercury, is combust the Sun. Combustion means that the brightness of the Sun overpowers the visibility of the object, rendering it difficult, if not impossible, to see. Thus, the keys are hidden or obscured by something. Kevin and his wife can’t see them, and the keys are likely behind, beneath, or within something that is blocking them from view. Because Mercury lies in Scorpio, a Water sign, the keys are low down wherever they are located.

The location of the missing item is shown by the 4th house and its ruler. Cancer rules the 4th house in this chart, and the Moon occupies Taurus in the 1st quadrant house, which is the 2nd Whole Sign house. Water signs indicate a position low down and possibly near water. The angular Moon suggests that the keys are nearby, possibly in the home or in a place frequently used by the querent. The 2nd house by Whole Sign suggests a place where valuables are normally stored or kept. Taurus, an Earth sign, has a connection to vegetation, parks, and gardens, so the keys may be near something used outdoors.

On Tuesday, 12 November 2019, Kevin wrote that he had found the keys by accident in his wife’s backpack at about 6:55 p.m., less than three hours after he asked the horary question. He discovered the missing keys as he was putting a cell phone in a smaller zipper pocket below the main compartment of the backpack. The Moon was at 25o Taurus (a little more than 1 degree away from Caput Algol), and the rising degree was approximately 13o–14o Gemini, which put Venus in her own term close to opposite the Ascendant degree. Kevin also noted that 2nd-ruler Mercury was in his own term/bound, indicating that the keys were still in good condition.

As the chart indicated, the missing keys were with his wife’s possessions in a nearby place where she would normally keep them. Given the predominance of Water signs, they were found in a pocket below the main compartment of the backpack. The fact that the chart has a late degree rising may reflect Kevin’s discovery of the keys by accident rather than by intentionally following the clues provided by the horoscope.
