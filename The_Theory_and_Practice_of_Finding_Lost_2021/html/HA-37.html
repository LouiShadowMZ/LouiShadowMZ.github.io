Chapter 37

BOYS MISSING IN JUPITER, FLORIDA

According to Lilly: “If a question be demanded of one absent in a general way, and the querent has no relation to the party; then the first House, the Lord of the House and the Moon shall signify the absent party; and the Lord of the 8th House or planet posited either in the House or within five degrees of the Cusp of the 8th House shall show his death or its quality”(CA 151).

On July 24, 2015, two teenage boys set out in their fishing boat from a marina in Jupiter, Florida, never to return. Apparently, an unexpected squall capsized their boat in the Atlantic Ocean close to the level of Florida’s border with Georgia. Air spotters reportedly saw a makeshift raft in the vicinity, but the boys were never found. As in the case in the previous chapter, we cast an event chart for the exact time that the missing fourteen-year-olds were last seen, namely, 24 July 2015 at 1:30 p.m. EDT, Jupiter, Florida (Chart 41a). It is a Venus day during a Sun hour. In such a chart, the Ascendant represents the missing boys.

This is a troubling chart. Scorpio rules the Ascendant, making Mars the significator of the missing teenagers. On the positive side, with Mars in the 9th house, Lilly’s dictum—namely, that the 1st house ruler in houses 9, 10, or 11 suggests that the missing person is probably alive (CA 151)—applies to this chart.

On the other hand, both the Ascendant and the Moon lie in the via combusta, an unlucky region associated with misfortune, deprivation of light, and matters being obscured or kept secret. Sahl says that Luna in the via combusta is one of the worst impediments of the Moon and is especially harmful in charts related to embarking on marriage, matters related to women, buying and selling, or long-distance travel (Dykes translation 2008, 194). Also in this chart, Mars (the missing persons) occupies the 9th house of long journeys and very closely applies to square Uranus (sudden accidents) in the unfortunate 6th house.

In astrology, the contra-antiscion of any point is its reflection across the axis connecting 0o Libra and 0o Aries. In other words, a point and its contra-antiscion are equally distant from the 0o Libra/0o Aries axis. Contra-antiscions act like stressful oppositions between planets. The contra-antiscion of radical Neptune closely conjoins Uranus in the 6th house (an accident at sea, possible drowning). Radical Neptune is disposed by Jupiter, which squares Saturn, ruler of the 4th house of final endings. The contra-antiscion of the Ascendant at 29o 23' Leo conjoins stationary Venus at 0o 45' Virgo, ruler of the 8th house of death and the 12th house of undoing. The contra-antiscion of the Sun, a natural signifier of life and vitality, conjoins radical Saturn (a natural symbol of death and ruler of the 4th house of endings) at the cusp of the 2nd house. The contra-antiscion of radical Saturn conjoins the radical Sun at the Midheaven, the most prominent point in the chart. Chart 41b shows the natal planets in the inside wheel, surrounded by their contra-antiscia in the outer wheel.

In addition, Mars conjoins the fixed star Castor, the mortal Gemini twin who died and went to heaven to be with his immortal brother. Some authors view Castor as an unfortunate fixed star of the nature of Saturn, Mars, and Venus. The Arabic parts file generated by Solar Fire reveals two significant contacts: the Part of Danger (3 o 02’ Leo) conjoins Mercury (ruler of the 9th house of sea voyages), and the Part of Catastrophe (19o 35’ Cancer) conjoins Ascendant-ruler Mars (the missing boys).

When I first looked at this chart, I hoped that through some miracle the boys would be found alive, but the chart suggested an unexpected accident in which the teens probably drowned and were washed out to sea. As the search proceeded, the Coast Guard found their nineteen-foot fishing boat about sixty-five miles east of Daytona Beach on July 26. The Coast Guard called off their search for the missing teens on 31 July 2015. In early August, two life jackets and a seat cushion allegedly belonging to the boys’ boat were found near Savannah, Georgia. A trained dog apparently detected the scent of one of the boys on the life jacket. The family called off their private search on 9 August 2015 and decided to set up a foundation to promote boat safety in honor of the lost boys.
