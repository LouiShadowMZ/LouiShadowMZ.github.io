Chapter 9

GADBURY: WHETHER THINGS
LOST WILL BE FOUND

The following are excerpts from Gadbury’s Doctrine of Horary Questions, Astrologically Handled (1658). I have modernized some of the seventeenth-century English to make it more readable. The first section deals with fugitives and strays. The second and third sections deal more generally with whether and when missing things or fugitives will be found.

Of the Fugitives or Strays, Shall They Be Found or Not?

1. The Moon and Mercury are naturally the Significators of Fugitives and strays: but the 7th House and his Lord particularly are Significators thereof; unless the thing strayed be a Beast, etc.

2. If the Lord of the 7th thereof shall be Retrograde, the fugitive shall return again of his own accord, before he goes very far from the Querent’s House, or from the place whence he went.

3. The Lord of the Ascendant or Moon, in good aspect with the Lord of the 7th, and the Lord of the 7th apply, it declares the fugitive to be coming home again. If he does not apply, the Querent may find him by enquiry.

4. But if the aforesaid Significators behold each other by square or opposition, or shall be in no aspect at all; it denotes the Fugitive unwilling to return again, and an unlikelihood of the Querent’s having him again: If the Lord of the 7th be in the 3rd or 9th, you may say the Fugitive is gone a journey, and will not suddenly be found again.

5. If a Horse, Ox or Cow, etc. [large animals] be strayed, observe the Lord of the 12th; and if he be found Retrograde, you may judge the stray will return of its own accord; and observe the same rules as before, only with the change of the Lord of your House.

6. If the Moon shall transfer the Light of the Lord of the Fugitive or Stray to the Lord of the Ascendant, it argues hopes of recovering them again. If she transfer Mercury’s Light or virtue, &c. it signifies the same.

7. The Moon in Good configuration with Jupiter or Venus, or either of them casting a good aspect to the Ascendant or his Lord, from good Houses of the Figure, shows hopes of a recovery.

8. The Lord of the 7th in the 12th House of the Figure or Question, denotes the Fugitive to be under the Bonds of restraint, perhaps in a Prison: Mercury posited so, argues the same.

9. When in the Figure you find any Planet separating from the Lord of the House of the Moon, the Beast, etc., strayed is taken into custody and driven away and sold: if any planet shall be found to separate from the Lord of the 2nd, the same.

10. If the Moon or Significator of the Stray, etc., shall apply to the Lord of the 8th from his Ascendant, or shall be in the 8th, it is then dead. If the Dispositor of the Moon, or Significator of the Beast shall be posited in the 8th House, applying to the square or opposition of an Infortune in the 4th, the same.

11. The Lord of the 6th or 12th, in the 6th or 12th, or in the 9th or 10th, argues the Beasts strayed to be either in the Pound, or under the Command of some Officer.

12. The Lord of the 7th, 12th, or 6th, fortunated by the good Beams of Jupiter or Venus in the 2nd, 5th or 11th Houses, or the Sun casting a trine unto them there, they are very likely to be found again. (Gadbury 1658, 279–280)

Of Things Lost, If Recoverable or Not?

1. Fortunate Planets in sextile or trine to the Lords of the Ascendant or 2nd House, and the Dispositor of the Part of Fortune posited in the Ascendant or 2nd House, declares recovery of the goods lost.

2. Either of the fortunes [Venus or Jupiter] in the Ascendant, having Dignities in the 2nd House, or the Moon in the 7th, in sextile or trine to the Lord of the Ascendant: both these are very certain Arguments of a recovery of the Thing or Things lost.

3. The Moon in the 10th, in trine to a Planet in the 2nd, or in the 2nd trine to the Lord of the 2nd or the Dispositor of the part of Fortune, the Lord of the Ascendant in the 2nd House; the lights in trine to each other or in trine to the Cusp of the 2nd House, or the Lord of the 2nd in the 11th or 4th Houses; all these are arguments of recovery.

4. The Lord of the 8th in the Ascendant, or with the Lord of the Ascendant, denotes a recovery of the Goods lost: Jupiter, Venus or North Node in the 11th House, give great hopes of the same also, and this I have often proved true.

5. The Moon, part of Fortune or his Dispositors, or Lord of the 2nd in the 8th House, is a great argument that the Goods lost cannot be recovered.

6. When both Luminaries are under the Earth, the Thing lost is hard to be recovered: If the 2nd House or his Lord be any way hindered, it denotes the same.

7. But the greatest argument of an irrecovery, are the position of Saturn, Mars or South Node out of their essential Dignities in the 2nd House; or the Lord of the 2nd being either in Combustion, or in the 8th House; or if the Lord of the 2nd be in square or opposition with the Lord of the 8th.

8. Mr. Lilly said If the Lord of the 7th shall be in conjunction with the Lord of the 8th; or if the Lord of the 2nd behold not the 1st House, or Lord thereof; or the Sun and Moon are not aspecting each other or the Part of Fortune; or when they are both under the Earth; there can be no restitution of the goods lost. (Gadbury 1658, 280–281)

In What Time Shall the Thing [Lost or] Stolen Be Recovered?

1. Seeing testimonies of recovery in your Figure, you may also discover the time thus: Observe the application of the two Planets signifying recovery, and consider the Degrees of distance between their body or Aspect, and so turn the distance into time.

2. If they happen to be in moveable [cardinal] signs, then give so many weeks or days as you have Degrees of distance, for the space, or time in which it may be recovered. If in common [mutable] signs, give months or weeks: If fixed signs, Years or Months.

3. When the Sun and Moon together behold the Ascendant, the Thing lost shall suddenly be recovered; for they suffer nothing to lie long hid, or obscured: And if at the same time, the Lord of the 2nd be in the Ascendant, it will be had again very speedily.

4. The Significators increasing in light and motion, and posited in fortunate places of the Figure, presage a sudden recovery of the Goods lost or missing. (Gadbury 1658, 282–283)
