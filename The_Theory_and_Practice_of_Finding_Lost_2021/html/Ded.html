This book is dedicated to the memory

of 17th-century astrologer William Lilly,
who valued reason and empirical evidence
over the unquestioning acceptance of tradition.

“Perhaps some will accuse me for dissenting from Ptolemy; I confess I have done so, and that I am not the first, or shall I that have done so, be the last; for I am more led by reason and experience, than by the single authority of any one man.”

—William Lilly, Christian Astrology, 1647
