Chapter 38

WHERE ARE THE MISSING USB DRIVES?

This question was asked by Del Strachen in a horary discussion group to which I belong. Del kindly gave his permission to use the chart in this text. Here is what he posted:

Hello this is my question, I am the Querent. I have misplaced my USB drives. Where are they? The date of me drawing the chart is September 10, 2015 at 8:09 a.m. AEST (-10:00), Sydney, Australia (33S52, 151E13).

Del cast the chart using Porphyry houses (Chart 42a). He found the USB drives sixteen minutes later, at 8:25 a.m., not long after studying the indications in the horary chart.

It is a Jupiter day during a Sun hour. An early degree of Scorpio rises, making Mars the ruler of the querent. The Ascendant lies in the via combusta.

In the Porphyry house system, Scorpio also rules the 2nd house cusp of his possessions, so Mars also rules the missing USB drives. If he had used Placidus or Regiomontanus houses, Sagittarius would have been on the 2nd cusp, making Jupiter the ruler of his movable goods; but since I was interpreting a chart he had already cast, I stuck with his choice of Porphyry houses.

The 2nd-ruler Mars in Leo (a Fire sign ruled by the Sun) suggests a location near heat or light. Sometimes Leo refers to an outdoor location such as a park. Mars close to the 11th house cusp suggests an 11th house location (perhaps with a friend or the child of a partner, 5th from the 7th). A position near the cusp can mean a location near a door, window, or threshold between rooms. Fire signs often suggest a location near a wall or partition.

Fiery Mars and sunny Leo suggest that the USB drives may be near something red or bright orange in color. Saturn (gravity) near the cusp of the 2nd house could mean he dropped them. Most of Sagittarius in the 2nd house suggests Jupiter as a co-ruler of the missing items. Jupiter in Virgo in the 11th house (the joy of Jupiter) could mean a location inside a drawer, pocket, cabinet, or container of some kind, possibly made of iron or steel (Mars in Leo). Virgo loves to organize and file things in neat compartments.

Jupiter (co-ruler of the missing items) in the house of his greatest joy (the 11th) indicates that the USBs are in good condition, and is also an argument that the USBs will be found, especially since the 11th house signifies the querent’s hopes and wishes in the matter. Jupiter under the sunbeams indicates that the USBs are likely obscured from view and not easily seen.

Saturn in Scorpio suggests a dark place. The Moon (co-ruler of the querent) applies to 2nd-ruler Mars, and Mars (ruler of the Ascendant) applies to Saturn at the cusp of the 2nd, so the missing items certainly will be found. Mars in a fixed sign suggests a hidden location low down in a room. Jupiter in a mutable Earth sign suggests it is in the house near the floor.

Del responded to my analysis of his horary chart as follows:

You are on the money. I have been moving my stuff from my old flat into storage. I am currently cat-sitting in a house that belongs to a woman with two kids. So I have been using a black storage container. It is about a meter from the front door. And it was next to a backpack that was off white but has an orange bit that is about half of the front. Yes, it was also on the floor. It was in a black storage container. I checked the storage container because I ran out of places to look.

Having read Del’s comment about cat-sitting, I noticed that Jupiter (ruler of the 6th house of small animals) lies in the 11th house of friends with Ascendant-ruler Mars (the querent). This kind of confirmatory detail is further evidence that the chart is radical.

Sometimes colors are helpful in locating lost objects. In this case, the 2nd house ruler is Mars, a red planet, in Leo (color of the Sun), and by the 5-degree rule, Mars is under the influence of the 11th house cusp (yellow or yellow-orange). The plurality of color testimonies suggests that the missing item is near something in the yellow-orange-red spectrum. It was actually in a black storage container, however, which is indicated by Jupiter in Virgo in the 11th house. Jupiter rules Sagittarius, which occupies most of the Porphyry 2nd house and is on the cusp of the Placidus and Regiomontanus 2nd house. Virgo often indicates a location in a drawer, container, or box, and is associated with very dark or black colors. Saturn in Scorpio on the cusp of the 2nd house also suggests a very dark or black color.

The dispositor of the Part of Fortune can co-signify the querent’s goods. Here, Fortuna in Virgo is ruled by Mercury in Libra in the 12th house. The Moon (the querent’s co-ruler) applies to sextile Mercury from the angular 10th house without impediment, suggesting recovery of the item.

Regarding the early degree on the Ascendant and the late degree on the 2nd Porphyry house cusp in the horary chart, we should consider the observations of Alphee Lavoie (1995) that the misplaced article may be

• near a door, window, or gate;

  • in a box, package, or container; and/or

    • about to be moved.

    In this case, Alphee’s findings are spot-on. The querent was in the process of moving his belongings, and he found the missing USBs in a storage container near the front door.

    Would it have made a difference if the astrologer had chosen to use Regiomontanus houses (Lilly’s favorite) instead of Porphyry? Here is the same horary cast with Regiomontanus houses (chart 42b).

    Mars still rules the Ascendant and signifies the querent, but Jupiter rules the 2nd house cusp and signifies the missing USBs. Jupiter in Virgo (a dark-colored or black container) in the 11th house of friends suggests a location in a friend’s house. The Moon (co-signifying the querent) lies at the end of the 10th house and is entering the 11th, suggesting movement of the querent from his own place to the location of a friend. By the 5-degree rule, the Moon would be regarded as being under the influence of the 11th house cusp; and none of the relevant significators would occupy an angular house, suggesting that the missing USBs may not be in the querent’s own home.

    It seems that either chart would lead to the same conclusions, but, at least in this case, the Regiomontanus chart is a little more direct than the one with Porphyry houses.
