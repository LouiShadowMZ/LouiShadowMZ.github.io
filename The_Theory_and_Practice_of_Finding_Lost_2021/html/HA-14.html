Chapter 14

THE HOUSES/PLACES (τóποι)

To interpret a horary chart correctly, the astrologer must be well versed in the meanings of the twelves houses of the horoscope. These twelve divisions of the zodiac originated in Hellenistic astrology, which regarded the rising sign as the first house, or “first place,” and numbered each subsequent whole sign in zodiacal order according to its “place” in the sequence from 1 to 12 (figure 3). Virtually all teachers of horary emphasize the need to understand the significations of the twelve houses, or places, as a prerequisite to analyzing the chart.

For example, Gadbury gives the following instructions on how to begin the interpretation of a horary chart: “Being well versed therein [in the 12 houses], observe the Nature of your Question propounded, and give the Moon, the Ascendant and his Lord, &c. to the Querent; then consider unto what House the person or thing enquired after belongs, and give the Moon, the Cusp of that House and his Lord, unto the thing or person enquired after” (Gadbury 1658, 236).

In the remainder of this chapter, I present some key attributions of the twelve houses, or places, of the horoscope, drawing on the literature from Hellenistic times to the present, including some observations from Hindu astrology. Because horary astrology attempts to assign all possible situations to just twelve houses, this compilation is necessarily incomplete, and the reader will want to amend, modify, and correct these proposed significations against the experience gained from working personally with a multitude of horary charts.

1st Place (Ascendant, the Helm)

The querent who asks the horary question; the body and appearance of the querent; the personality, temperament, life, health, vitality, survivability, length of life; the viability of a matter; a person inquired about with whom the querent has no personal connection; the missing person in a “last seen” event chart; the “safety or ruin” of a ship at sea (CA 157); the sick person in a decumbiture chart. Hellenistic astrologers referred to this house as the “helm” (steering mechanism).

LOCATIONS: On or near the querent’s person, with the querent, in a place often frequented by the querent, close by, near an entryway, with light-colored items, with items related to the head or face; in an area described by consignificators Aries and Saturn, or by Mercury, who rejoices in this house.

DIRECTION: East

COLORS: White, pale, wan, light gray

BODY PARTS: Head and face

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Aries and Saturn

JOY: Mercury (a general signifier of language and the tongue)

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKA: The Sun as signifier of self, vitality, the spark of life, well-being, viability, personal accomplishment, and physical constitution

2nd Place (Gates of Hades, Bios)

That which materially supports and sustains the life and vitality of the querent; movable possessions, personal assets, money, wealth, valuables, riches, gain, wages, substance, acquisitions, financial status, income, savings, profit and loss, money lent to others by the querent, the finances of the querent, resources, treasuries, movable goods, the cost or value of the querent’s assets; the death of a partner (8th from the 7th); one’s allies or “friends and assistants” in a lawsuit; one’s “second” in a duel. Anything that the querent owns, other than real estate, belongs in the 2nd house.

LOCATIONS: With one’s jewelry or valuables, where one stores possessions and movable goods, in a pocketbook or wallet, in a briefcase or backpack, in a safe or a bank, where the querent typically keeps money or important documents; in a closet, drawer, cabinet, cupboard, or coatroom; near greenish items, with things related to the throat or neck, in an area described by consignificators Taurus and Jupiter. If the significator of a missing object lies in the 2nd house, the item is likely to be in a place where you would normally keep it and you may simply have overlooked it. Go back and look again, especially if the significator is retrograde.

DIRECTION: East Northeast

COLOR: Green

BODY PARTS: The throat, neck, and the back of the neck toward the shoulders

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Taurus and Jupiter

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKA: Jupiter as signifier of wealth, finances, material goods, family, children, teachers, professions, gains, and resources. Indian astrology regards the planet ruling the 2nd house as a maraka that can indicate accidents, illness, poverty, misery, or even death for the native when activated in the dasha system.

3rd Place (Moon Goddess)

Brethren, siblings, relatives, cousins, kin, neighbors, short trips, change of location, inland journeys, short-distance travel; “travel, trotting and trudging” (CA 52); cars, vehicles used for local transport, letters, paperwork, written documents, communications of all types, studies, speeches, phone calls, mail, emails, rumors, messages, messengers, news, the press, written reports, newspapers, magazines, periodicals, journalists, the process of expressing things in writing. Ancient texts include religious sects and foreign travel in the 3rd house. Bonatti says the following in Treatise 6: On Questions: “Look at the 9th and its Lord, which signifies a long journey or pilgrimage (the 3rd a short journey, the 5th a journey of moderate length, the 12th the longest)” (Dykes 2010, 536). In summary, Bonatti (Dykes, 525) assigns journeys as follows:

• 3rd house: short journeys (no more than one day)

• 5th house: journeys of moderate length (from one to three days)

• 9th house: long journeys and pilgrimages (from three to sixty days)

• 12th house: the longest journeys of all (sixty days or longer)

LOCATIONS: With mail or papers, a study room, where writing is done, near a computer, on a table or desk, in a desk drawer or file cabinet, in an office; near a telephone, computer, keyboard, or communication device; with a written deed or contract; with relatives, siblings, or a neighbor; in a car or vehicle, in a garage, near a staircase; nearby, in one’s neighborhood, at the post office, with things related to shipping or transport, in a suitcase used for travel; with saffron- or chestnut-colored items, with things related to the shoulders or upper extremities; in an area described by consignificators Gemini and Mars, or by the Moon, who rejoices in this house. Lilly links the 3rd house to “removing from one place to another” (CA 52), so the missing item may have changed locations and thus may not be where the querent thinks it is or should be.

DIRECTION: North Northeast

COLORS: Red and yellow, croceal (the orange-yellow color of the autumn crocus, aka meadow saffron), saffron, sorrel (light reddish brown or chestnut-colored)

BODY PARTS: Shoulders, arms, hands, and fingers

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Gemini and Mars

JOY: The Moon (the fastest-moving celestial body, associated with travel, change, and wandering people and things); the 3rd is the ancient house of the moon goddess, as opposed to the 9th house of the sun god.

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKA: Mars as a signifier of siblings, decisiveness, courage, motivation, energy, initiative, mental or physical prowess, starting something new, and determination to pursue goals

4th Place (Subterranean Place)

Parents, fathers, elders, ancestry, heritage, roots, antiques, buried or hidden treasure, property, real estate, houses, ancient dwellings, very old things, the end of matters, the grave, postmortem affairs, the verdict in court cases, successions (legal entitlements to the title or property of the deceased), the querent’s home or residence, an automobile as one’s home away from home, family property, soil, the land, immovable goods, buildings, apartments, tenements, castles, villas, cities and towns, lands, the countryside, gardens, vineyards, grounds, fields, orchards, pastures, farms, crops, farming, gardening, agriculture, dirt, tillage of the earth, the condition of the land and its ability to produce crops, the weather, resources beneath the surface of the earth, wells, mines, mining, valuable resources buried in the ground, things mislaid, covered and concealed things; this is the bottommost and darkest place in the chart.

LOCATIONS: In the home or at a property belonging to a family member, the oldest part of the house or where an older person spends a lot of time, a family room, where things are rooted or have their foundation, in the basement, with parents or elders, ancestral places, with reddish items, buried in the earth (subterranean), on the floor, at the bottom of a location, with things used for growing plants (e.g., in a garden or greenhouse), in a field or garden, in a safe place where the querent might typically “bury treasure”; with items related to the breast and lungs, in an area described by consignificators the sign Cancer and the Sun. For a missing ship, the 4th house could signify the bottom of the ocean.

DIRECTION: North

COLOR: Red

BODY PARTS: Breast, chest, and lungs

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Cancer and the Sun

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKAS: The Moon as a signifier of the mother, the mind, feelings, mothering, comfort, and emotional happiness. Indian astrologers place one’s vehicle or conveyance in the 4th house, possibly because our cars function much like our mother’s womb in carrying us about. They also link Mercury to the 4th house as a symbol of education, perhaps because the mind (symbolized by the Moon) requires education for its proper development.

5th Place (Good Fortune)

Love, courtship, love affairs, fun, pleasures, recreation, things that make you feel good, luxuries, adornments, fancy clothing, games, places of amusement, delights, reproductive sex, babies, offspring, children, pregnancy, breastfeeding, circumcision, things done for fun or entertainment, where one spends enjoyable time with friends or lovers, parties, feasts, fiestas, vacations, pleasure trips, gifts, hobbies, bodily pleasures and indulgences, the “finer things in life,” artistic enjoyment, rich foods, delicious meals, banquets, taverns, restaurants, bars, nightclubs, theaters, plays, theatrical performances, places of recreation including parks and public gardens, sports, sporting arenas, merriment, speculation, betting, lotteries, recreational alcohol and drug use, gambling, speculative investments; the parents’ wealth and movable goods (2nd from 4th); the assets of a 4th house city or town; profits from real estate (2nd from 4th); the friends of a spouse or partner (11th from 7th); couriers, pigeons, and ambassadors who are sent by and return to the querent; writing documents to be sent; journeys of one to three days’ duration (Bonatti). Modern astrologers link the 5th house to creative and artistic endeavors, perhaps because Venus, the goddess of love and adornment, rejoices here. For example. John Frawley regards personal artistic creations, including books that you write, to be metaphorical 5th house babies. In contrast, Lilly placed books in the 9th house (CA 55).

The theme of many 5th house significations appears to be “if it feels good, do it.” Note that lovers are signified by the 7th house, lovemaking by the 5th, and the sex organs (“privy parts”) by the 8th.

LOCATIONS: Places used for entertainment or recreation, a child’s room, locations where you have fun, a space related to a hobby or enjoyable activity, sports fields, gyms, areas used for romance or sexual pleasure, where one shares a good time with others (e.g., restaurants, bars, theaters, stadiums, concert halls, spas, parks, public gardens, living rooms, playrooms, etc.); with objects of beauty, comfort, or adornment; with new or fashionable clothing; where the querent’s parents keep their valuables; with honey-colored items; with things related to the heart, stomach, liver, sides, and back; in an area described by consignificators Leo and Venus, who rejoices in this house.

DIRECTION: North Northwest

COLORS: Black and white (dark gray?), honey, amber, golden

BODY PARTS: Heart, stomach, liver, sides, and back

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Leo and Venus (Venus rejoices in the 5th.)

JOY: Venus (the love goddess who enjoys luxury, elegance, finery, beauty, fashion, sensual indulgence, and good times)

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKA: Jupiter as signifier of children, progeny, generativity, creative self-expression, spiritual practices, intelligence, sound advice, luck, risk-taking, and karma from a previous reincarnation

6th Place (Bad Fortune)

Sickness, infirmities, bodily affliction, illness, remedies, conflict; day laborers, servants, subordinates, inferiors, servitude, “galley slaves” (CA 53), those who toil at menial labor or thankless tasks for the querent’s benefit; pets, small animals, and the profit or loss from raising “lesser cattle” (up to the size of goats); those whom the querent employs to provide a service; farmers, shepherds, gamekeepers; vassals, tenants; the parent’s siblings (3rd from 4th), the death of a friend (8th from 11th). Some astrologers include modern devices that serve us by slavishly doing our drudge work (e.g., computers, domestic appliances, etc.) in the 6th house. Lilly (CA 52) views the main significations of the 6th house as bodily ailments; servants and galley slaves; small animals and the profit or loss from herding, “lesser cattle” (sheep, hogs, rabbits, goats, etc.); the father’s siblings (3rd from 4th); and the “inferior part of the belly and intestines even to the arse.” Lilly includes pets in the 6th house: “the Sign of the 6th and its Lord signify the Dog, so they must have done if it had been a Sheep, or Sheep, Hogs, Conies, &c., or any small Cattle” (CA 393). (Perhaps “tenants” are included in the 6th because they are people herded together like lesser cattle to benefit a master.)

LOCATIONS: In a sickroom, in a place related to medicine or where medicines are kept, in a doctor’s office or pharmacy, in the workplace, where menial labor is done, a servant’s quarters, where tools or small appliances are kept, a workshop or utility room, where routine tasks or chores are done, with or near an employee or subordinate, with a tenant, on a table or desk, where secretarial work is done, in a folder or file cabinet, in a drawer or container, with a child’s valuables (2nd from 5th), with an aunt or uncle, with a pet, where “lesser cattle” are herded, with dark-colored items, with things related to the lower belly or intestines; in an area described by consignificators Virgo and Mercury, or by Mars, who rejoices in this house. The 6th house is inconjunct (quincunx, or in aversion to) the Ascendant and signifies places that the querent cannot see, as well as changes or travel from place to place; thus, the missing item may be in a location other than where the querent thinks it is or should be, or else it may be concealed behind, within, or beneath something that blocks the view.

DIRECTION: West Northwest

COLORS: Dark colors, blackish

BODY PARTS: Lower belly and the intestines “even to the arse” (CA 54)

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Virgo and Mercury

JOY: Mars (the god of war and a general signifier of fevers, acute illnesses, piercing wounds, burns, and inflammations)

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKAS: Mars as signifier of enemies, adversaries, competitors, how well you are able to compete, hostilities, litigation (legal battles), accidents, acute illness, inflammation, injuries, and theft; also Saturn as a symbol of debt, service, servants, hard work, menial tasks, hardship, and disease

7th Place (Setting Place)

Sunset, nightfall, marriage, marital issues, spouses, lovers, sexual partners, coital interactions, sweethearts, business partners, partnerships, coming-together, negotiations, matters involving two people, pacts, contracts, legally binding agreements, the other party in a contract, the defendant in a lawsuit; colleagues, acquaintances, “one you familiarly deal with or are much conversant with, and is not allied to you” (by blood or other specific relationship); adversary, competitor, public enemy, opponent; duels, lawsuits, contentions, board games (like chess), war, divorce, robbers, fugitives, outlaws, escaped convicts, runaways; paid consultants (including the querent’s personal physician or astrologer), the other party in a financial agreement; the place one would remove to (if you leave your current residence and relocate to a new one); the destination of a journey (“the land for which he set out”—Bonatti, 526); factors that threaten the integrity or viability of whatever is signified by the Ascendant, which lies opposite across the wheel.

Lilly writes (CA 147): “The ascendant and his lord are for the querent; the seventh house and his lord for him whom you would speak withal; this is understood, if you go to speak with one you familiarly deal with or are much conversant with, and is not allied to you” [by blood or other special relationship]—however, for the father, use the 4th; for the mother, the 10th; for a child, the 5th; and so on.

LOCATIONS: With a partner or other person who may or may not have been involved in misplacing the item; where a spouse or partner spends a lot of time or keeps their valuables; in the home but in a place other than, “contrary to,” or “exiled from” where the querent usually keeps the missing item (the 7th house is opposite the 1st, which signifies the querent); with something black or in a dark or obscure location, having fallen into a dark place like the setting sun at dusk; with things or in locations related to the region of the body from the navel to the buttocks (e.g., under a chair, beneath or alongside a car seat, etc.); in an area described by consignificators Libra and the Moon.

DIRECTION: West

COLOR: Black

BODY PARTS: Haunches (buttock and thigh considered together); the region from the navel to the buttocks, which includes the genital area

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Libra and the Moon

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKAS: Venus as signifier of women, wives, finery, partnerships, mutual relations, sexual partners, and conjugal life. Also, Jupiter as a symbol of the husband in a marriage-like or intimate relationship, as well as signifier of long trips and matters related to foreign countries, including residence abroad or far from home. (Indian astrology regards the planet ruling the 7th house as a maraka that can indicate accidents, illness, poverty, misery, or even death for the native when activated in the dasha system. This view is consistent with the “setting sun” symbolism of the 7th house.)

8th Place (Idle Place)

Death, killing, loss, concealment, elimination, anxiety, fear, dread, stress, panic, anguish of mind, idleness, sluggishness, debt, decline, decay, decomposition, waste, trash, refuse, goods of the dead, undertakers, last will and testament, legacies, dowry, assets or valuables of a partner, money borrowed by the querent from others, resources of the other party in a financial agreement, allies or assets of the querent’s opponents, hiding places, secrets, the occult, the unseen, that which is obscured and needs to be uncovered to be revealed and understood, the privy parts (the genitals and, by analogy, things covered or hidden so they cannot easily be seen), crises, unexpected or unforeseen situations, lethal poisons, serious medical or surgical interventions to deal with life-threatening situations. The 8th house is inconjunct (in aversion to) the Ascendant and signifies places that the querent cannot see; thus, the missing item may be in a location other than where the querent thinks it ought to be, or else it may be concealed behind, within, or under something that blocks the view. For example, in a chart about an escaped prisoner (CA 470), Lilly notes that the prisoner’s significator in the 8th means that “he lay obscurely for a while,” that is, he hid himself from view to avoid being caught.

LOCATIONS: Places where people or things can “lie obscurely” (CA 470), locations that are protected or hidden from view, banks, safe deposit boxes, wallets, pocketbooks, pockets in clothing, secret areas, hiding places, closets, covered or concealed locations, “idle” places that are visited infrequently; with a partner’s goods, where a partner or someone who lives with the querent keeps money or valuables (2nd of 7th), such as a purse or piggy bank; in the home of the querent’s child (radical 8th is the 4th from the 5th of children), with refuse or junk, in the trash, out in the garbage or recycling; a place where one works with dirt or waste, a laundry room, washroom, bathroom, water closet; with things related to the genitals or the bladder, in the underwear drawer; funeral parlors, morgues, locations related to surgery or healing practices, places associated with the dead or the afterlife; near something dark or heavy (saturnine), with dark green items; in an area described by consignificators Scorpio and Saturn (e.g., a dark, dirty, moist, or shaded place); in the bathroom, or in places connected with the bladder, genitals, anus, or feces (e.g., under a sofa or car seat). Modern astrologers link the 8th house, Scorpio, and Pluto with death and ideas of transformation and rebirth (or changes to the usual pattern of one’s life), so the missing object could be in a place undergoing demolition and renovation.

DIRECTION: Southwest

COLORS: Green and black (dark green)

BODY PARTS: Specifically, the sex organs (the “privy parts,” CA 54); also the bladder, anus, and organs of elimination

CONSIGNIFICATORS: Scorpio and Saturn

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKA: Saturn (the final visible planet) as signifier of fear, loss, worries, mental anguish, chronicity, limiting conditions, longevity, old age, endings, finality, death, the fickle finger of fate (sudden events), and matters that are dark or hidden, including the occult

9th Place (Sun God)

Religion, spirituality, sacred matters, religious rites, church weddings, preaching, philosophy, divine wisdom, dreams, visions, astrology, foresight, worship of a deity, pilgrimages, long journeys (up to sixty days in length, according to Bonatti), long-distance travel, voyages, travel abroad, foreigners, foreign lands, unfamiliar environments, exploration, teaching, higher knowledge, places where knowledge is stored, expert opinion, science, advanced learning, teachers, gurus, scholars, learned people, scholarship, jurisprudence, lawyers (CA 403, 403, 630), legal advice, expanding one’s horizons, the clergy, religious or devout persons, churches, church weddings, formal or official wedding services, temples, mosques, monasteries, ecclesiastical preferment, dreams, visions, prophesy, oracles, divination, tarot reading, foreign lands, libraries, universities, books (CA 55), publishing, diffusion of knowledge, rumors, a partner’s siblings or kin (3rd from the 7th), grandchildren (5th from the 5th).

JOY: Saturn (the “author of mischief,” CA 56)

JYOTISH NAISARGIK KARAKAS: Saturn as a signifier of loss, endings, expenses, sorrow, isolation, things done in private, restriction, darkness, impediments, obstacles, confinement, hospitalization, imprisonment, renunciation, and exile (removal to a foreign land). Also, Ketu (South Lunar Node) is a symbol of dissolution, liberation, and letting go.

1. Networking is a modern concept. I used this word here to signify what Al-Rijal (aka Haly Abenragel, a tenth-century Arab astrologer) refers to as seeking friendship to earn assets or to profit from real estate, which seems to me to mean cultivating business contacts or networking. See Dykes’s translation of Choices and Inceptions (p. 374). Perhaps this ancient signification is also the source of the modern attribution of groups, clubs, and societies or associations to which the querent belongs as matters of the 11th house.
