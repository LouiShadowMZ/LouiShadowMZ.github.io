Appendix V

HOW TO CALCULATE
DWADS, OR 12TH PARTS

Twelfth parts, or dwads, represent an interesting technique that can be added to the horary astrologer’s armamentarium. The online site Astro-Seek.com offers the option to calculate dwads. For those who would like to do it by hand, here is a simple method to compute 12th parts with a calculator or spreadsheet. The idea is that you are projecting a single sign of 30 degrees onto the entire zodiac of 12 signs and 360 degrees.

Assign the zodiac sign of a planet or point to a number from 1 to 12, starting with Aries as 1. The sequence is as follows:

1—Aries

5—Leo

9—Sagittarius

2—Taurus

6—Virgo

10—Capricorn

3—Gemini

7—Libra

11—Aquarius

4—Cancer

8—Scorpio

12—Pisces

Convert the degrees and minutes to decimal format, noting that there are 60 minutes in each degree. Thus 23 degrees 17 minutes becomes:

23 + (17 ÷ 60) = 23.2833

Multiply this number by 12 to project its position in its sign onto the entire 12 signs of the zodiac:

23.2833 × 12 = 279.3996

Divide this number by 30, because there are 30 degrees in each sign of the zodiac:

279.3996 ÷ 30 = 9.3133

The integer part of the number before the decimal point shows how many signs you need to move forward in the zodiac. If the sum comes to greater than 12, subtract 12 to get the number of the new sign.

Multiply the number of signs by 30, then subtract that product from the previous number:

9 × 30 = 270

279.3996 − 270 = 9.3996 degrees into the new sign

Masha’allah on Hidden Things

In his essay On Hidden Things (Dykes translation, 2008), Masha’allah discusses various factors in the chart that can reveal the querent’s hidden concerns and intentions in seeking an astrological consultation. Of all the methods he has tested, he says the most reliable is the 12th part (dwad) of the Ascendant of the consultation chart. Masha’allah proceeds as follows:

1. The sign and house occupied by the dwad of the Ascendant. Begin by considering the zodiacal sign of the 12th part of the Ascendant degree. If any planet occupies that sign, then the querent’s intentions will be related to the place (house) that sign represents in the horoscope.

Chart 48: Masha’allah on Hidden Things, Hypothetical Chart
4:42:21 p.m. LMT, 14 October 788, Baghdad, Iraq.
Alcabitius houses. Dwad of Ascendant is at 18o Leo.

2. The house occupied by the planetary ruler of the sign of the 12th part of the Ascendant. If the sign of the 12th part is not occupied by any of the visible planets, then the place occupied by the ruler of that sign (the dispositor of the 12th part of the Ascendant) will reveal what is on the querent’s mind.

Masha’allah gives the following example, for which I constructed a hypothetical chart to match his description (Chart 48).

Masha’allah tells us that a man came to consult him at a time when the Ascendant was in the 12th degree of Aries. Estimating the degree of the Ascendant to be 11o 30' Aries, I calculated the 12th part to be 18o Leo. Because Leo is the 5th Whole Sign from the Ascendant, Masha’allah deduced that the querent was concerned about a child.

Next, seeing that the 5th Whole Sign was not occupied by any visible planet, Masha’allah looked to the placement of the Sun, which rules Leo and the 5th house in this chart. Because the Sun, ruler of the 5th house of children, lies in the 7th house, Masha’allah said to the querent that “the question was about a child [the son of the querent] who sought a woman, wanting to take her as his wife. And if the Sun had been in the 6th, I would have said that he was seeking concerning an infirm child, and so on with the rest of the twelve signs” (Masha’allah, Dykes translation, 2008, 428).
