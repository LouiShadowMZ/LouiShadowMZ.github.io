WHAT HAPPENED TO THE GIRL?

According to news reports, on 21 September 2013, a young couple found the dead body of a twelve-year-old girl in a wooded area a few miles from her family’s cottage. The corpse turned out to be that of a local girl who had been adopted from China at age one by a wealthy family in Spain. The police investigation revealed that she had recently been texting friends that she thought her parents were trying to kill her, perhaps by poisoning her with drugs. Teachers reported that the girl had come to class appearing drugged, confused, and sleepy on a few occasions.

The mother told police that she allegedly last saw her daughter on Saturday, 21 September 2013, at 7:00 p.m. However, the police found witnesses who had seen the girl with her father at 6:18 p.m., and they also recovered video footage showing the girl with her mother in her car at 6:22 p.m. Because the police caught the parents in a series of demonstrable lies, they accused the parents of murdering the child. When she died, the girl was a week and a half away from her thirteenth birthday. The autopsy confirmed that she had been drugged with benzodiazepines over an extended period, and she had a high level of the drugs in her system on the night she died.

Here is the chart, with Regiomontanus houses, for the last sighting of the girl on 21 September 2013 at 6:22 p.m. CEDT in Santiago de Compostela, Spain (chart 30).

Aquarius rises, making Saturn the significator of the missing girl. Because Saturn lies in the 9th house, Lilly’s dictum—namely, that the 1st house ruler in houses 9, 10, or 11 suggests that the missing person is probably alive (CA 151)—applies to this chart. Unfortunately, Saturn is closely conjunct the Moon’s nodes, which are considered “fateful” degrees and give a more ominous indication. In addition, Saturn at 9o 02' Scorpio lies in the via combusta, suggesting a matter that is obscured (deprived of light), “burnt,” or being kept secret.

The Moon is the girl’s co-significator. Planets in the 1st house also describe the missing person. Neptune is conjunct Chiron, and both are retrograde, which may indicate that she has been drugged, poisoned, or wounded in some way.

The Moon’s Nodes, Their “Bendings,” and the Workings of Fate

As mentioned, Saturn (the girl’s signifier) lies in Scorpio in the 9th house and is conjunct the Moon’s mean North Node. Some horary astrologers believe that the degree of the Moon’s nodes implies a fated, though not necessarily fateful, event that is for some reason outside of the querent’s control. Such happenings may involve being in the wrong place at the right time, or some permutation of this popular phrase. Often significators in a nodal degree imply that what will happen is either in the hands of someone else or is due to the querent’s automatic or habitual patterns of behavior undertaken without consideration of potential consequences (Figure 7).

Ivy Goldstein-Jacobson wrote in an ominous tone that “any planet or angle in the same degree as the nodes points to a catastrophe, casualty, fatality or tragedy in a horary or natal chart, the more far-reaching when a malefic is involved” (Goldstein-Jacobson 1960, 52). She used the Moon’s mean nodal positions rather than the so-called “true” lunar nodes, which are calculated to include the effect of the Sun’s gravity on the Moon’s orbit around the Earth. Olivia Barclay agreed with Goldstein-Jacobson that any planet in the degree of the nodes is unfortunate. (Barclay 1990, 105)

The lunar nodes are the points of intersection of the apparent (geocentric) orbits of the Sun and Moon around the Earth. The “bendings” occur at the points where the orbits of the Sun and the Moon are most separated (in a north-south direction) from each other.

The use of the lunar nodes in this way in horary astrology appears to be a modern idea not found in Lilly’s works or other traditional sources. Nonetheless, the lunar nodes in traditional astrology were important as places where eclipses occurred, and eclipses were universally feared as malevolent omens because they blocked the life-giving light of the Luminaries and allowed evil forces to emerge from the darkness.

Goldstein-Jacobson and Barclay may have confounded the meanings of fated and fateful in their interpretations of nodal degrees. I have seen several horary charts with significators in nodal degrees where matters turned out well. See, for example, the example of Molly’s missing voice recorder in Chapter 1. In Molly’s horary chart (Chart 3), both the Ascendant ruler and the 2nd house ruler lie in degrees of the Moon’s mean nodes, yet she quickly found the missing device and it was in good condition. Perhaps the nodal degree in this case implies that it was Molly’s destiny to misplace the recorder so that she could write about it in her astrology blog. In her horary chart, the North Node lies in Pisces in the 3rd house of writing. Her Ascendant-ruler Jupiter disposes the North Node. Neptune, the modern ruler of Pisces, conjoins the 3rd house cusp of communications. In addition, the Aquarius 3rd house cusp has as its traditional ruler Saturn, which lies in Leo in the 9th house of publishing.

Rather than the degree of the Moon’s mean nodes being universally unfortunate, as Barclay believes, I suspect that it has more to do with the individual’s fate, karma, destiny, or habitual unconscious patterns of behavior. An event can be “fated” without being fateful or catastrophic. Fated simply means subject to, guided by, or determined by fate rather than by the querent’s conscious will. A fated event is one that is beyond the querent’s active intentional control. Such events can be either favorable or unfavorable. In addition, fate is often of our own making as we repeat habitual patterns from our past, going with the flow rather than actively seeking expert guidance to learn more effective ways to accomplish our goals.

In the girl’s “last seen” chart (Chapter 30), Saturn in the nodal degree is highly significant because Saturn conjoins the Moon’s North Node, rules the Ascendant (the querent), and also rules the 12th house of secret enemies and undoing. Interestingly, Saturn occupies Scorpio, a sign associated with poisoning.

In addition, Pluto (at the cusp of the unfortunate 12th house) squares the Moon’s nodes, a condition often called “being at the bendings” of the nodes. A planet at the bendings can represent a crisis or turning point in the querent’s life. The bendings are the points on the Moon’s orbit where it reaches maximum distance from the Sun’s orbit and changes direction from north to south, or south to north, with respect to the path of the Sun. Being at the bendings is analogous to the Sun changing direction from north to south, or south to north, at the summer and winter solstices.

As Kevin Burk notes in his Complete Node Book:

In classical astrology, a planet that was square the nodes was said to be at the bendings, and this planet would become a focal point of change and crisis for the individual. (Burk 2003, 212)

One experienced astrologer, Astrojin at Skyscript.co.uk, delineates a planet that is in square aspect to the Moon’s nodes as follows:

When a planet is conjoined to the lunar bendings (square the nodes), it can mean crisis due to unwelcome change or things will bend in the direction that is away from that which is proper. (Astrojin 2011)

I would add that the change symbolized by the bendings may not necessarily be “unwelcome” or “improper” but may simply be something unexpected, not customary, or different from the habitual way things have been happening up to that point.

In addition, in this chart, the Moon is void of course, according to both the modern definition and Lilly’s criteria. The Moon is in a kind of limbo state, because she is not applying to any planet from her current position and will not perfect any aspect before leaving Aries. Lilly says of the VOC Moon:

You shall seldom see a business go handsomely forward when she is so. (CA 112)

The Moon is also peregrine (in a place where she has no essential dignity) at her position in Aries, suggesting that the girl is without protection, assistance, or support.

At this point we can say with some confidence that the girl (Ascendant-ruler Saturn) may be involved in some fateful event over which she has little or no control (Saturn in a lunar nodal degree). Furthermore, the matter of the girl will not go handsomely forward (Moon VOC in Aries, where it is without dignity) and may end in an undesired manner. We want to find the girl alive, but the VOC Moon suggests that this matter is not likely to go handsomely forward.

The last aspect made by the Moon was an opposition to Mercury, ruler of the 8th and occupying the 8th house of death. The Moon (co-ruler of the girl and a general signifier of missing persons) will enter within orb of opposing Saturn before she leaves Aries. Saturn is afflicted, being conjunct a lunar node and ruling the 12th house of undoing and, through exaltation, the 8th house of death (Libra is intercepted in the 8th house, and Saturn is exalted in Libra). In addition, Mercury rules the 8th cusp of death, and Mercury lies in Libra, which is ruled by Venus and where Saturn is exalted.

The Moon (co-ruler of the girl) begins to apply to oppose Saturn toward the end of Aries and goes on to perfect an opposition in early Taurus to Saturn and then to Venus. Saturn rules her 12th house of secret enemies and, by exaltation, her 8th house of death. Venus rules Libra, which is intercepted in the 8th house of death. Venus also rules the end-of-the-matter 4th house, which also signifies her parents.

Traditional horary astrologers consider only the five major Ptolemaic aspects. A modern astrologer, in contrast, would note the closely applying quincunx from the Moon in Aries (the missing person) to the Sun in Virgo at the cusp of the 8th house of death. The quincunx, as a modern aspect, can be considered to have 6th and 8th house significance (sickness, difficult adjustments, and death) because it involves two zodiac signs that are either six or eight signs apart.

Finally, I should mention that this is an event chart for the time the girl was last seen rather than a horary chart asked by someone personally involved in the case. I have noticed that in “last seen” event charts in which the person is eventually found dead, the ruler of the 8th house is frequently conjunct but separating from the ruler of the Ascendant. (Sometimes the rulers of the Ascendant and the 8th house are separating from a square or opposition that is still within orb.) In horary charts, a separating aspect refers to events that have already transpired. In “last seen” event charts for missing persons, a conjunction, square, or opposition that is still within orb between the Ascendant ruler and the 8th house ruler implies that the missing person is dead, even if the two significators are separating. In this girl’s case, Venus rules the 8th house, because Libra is intercepted in the 8th house of death, and Venus is separating from a conjunction with Ascendant-ruler Saturn.

All in all, this “last seen” chart fairly well describes the murder of this unfortunate adopted girl from China.
