Chapter 3

PUOTINEN’S BASIC GUIDELINES (1989)

In the very first issue of The Horary Practitioner (April 1989), astrologer C. J. Puotinen, D.F.Astrol.S., presented an article entitled “Lost & Found: The Basic Rules.” Because this journal is out of print and may be hard to find, I will summarize here some of the highlights from her article with direct quotes:

• Identify the significators. “Before you can find something lost, you have to define exactly what it is so you can know what significators to use.” (p. 5)

• The chart must describe the situation. “The charts that work best are those that describe the situation accurately.” (p. 6)

• Signifiers of the missing object. “In addition to being shown by the 2nd ruler, a missing item is represented by the Moon and the ruler of the planetary hour. … If the significator is in the 1st house, or in the same sign as the 1st ruler, or in a sign ruled by the 1st ruler, the missing thing is with the querent or in the part of the querent’s house that he uses most often, or where he keeps his own possession or favorite things.” (p. 8) [A similar principle applies to the other angular houses. For example, the 10th can signify one’s office or workspace; the 7th, where the partner spends a lot of time; and the 4th, the oldest part of the house or where an older person spends time; and so on.]

• The significator in a very early or late degree. “If the significator is at the very end or beginning of a sign, the missing item may be near a door or entryway, or between two rooms, or it may have fallen behind something.” (p. 9)

• Significators in the same quadrant of the chart. “If the Moon is in the same quadrant as the 1st ruler and there is less than one sign (30o) between them, the lost thing is in or about the house of the person who lost it. If they are separated by 30o–70o, it is nearby or in the owner’s town. If they are not in the same quadrant, it is far from the owner.” (p. 9)

• Most recent aspect of the Ascendant ruler. “The planet from which the significator of the person who lost it separates may describe what happened.” (p. 10)

• Non-recovery. “The chief signs of no recovery are Saturn, Mars or the South Lunar Node in the 2nd house, the 2nd ruler in the 8th house or combust, or the 2nd ruler applying to the 8th ruler with any aspect.” (p. 13)
