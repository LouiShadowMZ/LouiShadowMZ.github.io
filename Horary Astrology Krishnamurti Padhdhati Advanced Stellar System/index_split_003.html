HINDU TRADITIONAL SYSTEM

TRADITIONAL SYSTEM OF HINDUS

Let me first of all furnish the translation of various textbooks on Horary Astrology written by Hindu sages., before I put forth my discovery. The readers should also know what the Westerners have propounded.

Prasna Gyana

Bhattotpala is the author of this work. He had commented on Varaha Mihira's works and this text deals with one of the branches of Astrology called Horary Astrology.

 

Text-books of our sages can give only the fundamental principles. The result entirely depends on the intelligent anticipation by proper application and interpretation by the astrologer without taking into consideration one's desire. Wishful thoughts and guess will prove to be useless.

 

Prasna Gyana —— Translation of the Text.

Sloka 2: He boldly declares that Horary Astrology will never fail. He expects the astrologers to carefully judge the ten conditions of the planets after casting their exact position.

 

The ten conditions of the planets are given in Jataka Parijata and their results are explained in Saravali.

Planets in their own sign indicate pleasure, happiness, wealth like gold, gems, etc. and one can have servants at command.

 

(Planets in its own sign equally causes miseries and do harm also; it is not a guarantee that such a planet should invariably do good. It depends on the lord of the constellation and the lord of the sub.)

 

(c) Pramudhita: Satisfied, contented: A planet is said to be satisfied when it occupies its friend's quarters. One can find out the friend's quarters by using the following:

 

Swa, Swanthya, Dhi, Dharma, Swechcha, Ayu, Sukha, L.e.. count from the Moolatrikona sign of the planet whose friendly sign is to be found. The first, second, twelfth, fifth, ninth, exaltation sign eighth and fourth counted from the Moolatrikona signs are the friendly signs. The other signs are said to be enemical, i.e., 3, 6, 10, 11 and 7. One can easily remember that the seventh and the upachaya Sthanas counted from Moolatri- kona signs are not friendly ones. The lords of the friendly signs are called the friends of the planet; the lords of the signs 3, 6, 10, 11 and 7 are said to be the enemy of that planet. It may happen that one of the two signs owned by a planet is friendly and the other is enemical.

 

The authors say that such a planet owning both the friendly and enemical signs should be considered as neutral, e.g. take Sun. Its Moolatrikona sign is Leo. Therefore Mercury owning Gemini and Virgo becomes the lord of the 11th and the second sign counted from Sun's Moolatrikona sign. 11th is enemical. 2nd is friendly. So Mercury is neutral to Sun; 3rd sign Libra is owned by Venus which also rules Taurus the 10th sign from Leo, the Moolatrikona of sign of Sun, Leo. As the 3 and 10 are enemicals Venus is the enemy of Sun. Take the 4th sign Scorpio. It is owned by Mars who also governs Aries, the 9th sign. Therefore Mars is friendly with Sun. Thus one is to find out the friendship, enmity and neutral condition of the planets.

 

Such a planet causes contentment; it offers peace; it is also said that one may have damsels, riches like gems and precious metals, enjoy one's life and come out successful in one's enterprise and victorious in the disputes. (Just like friends turn out to be rogues causing danger to the person, so also planets in friend's quarters offer undesirable results to many.)

 

(d) Santha: Calm, tranquil; If a planet is in the Varga of a benefic, it is said to be Santha'; by Benefics, one is to mean benefics by nature, i.e. Jupiter, Venus, Waxing Moon, and unafflicted Mercury. Such a planet in Santha condition shows that the person will be helpful. He or she will do meritorious deeds. They will be educated and learned. They can become advisers and ministers. Hence they can enjoy life with enough financial position and have peace.

 

(e) Saktha: Capable, competent, efficient. A planet is in Saktha condition, if it is not Moudya, Mooda and eclipsed but is with bright rays. Such a planet will bring one name, fame, reputation, honour etc. He will be liked by all. He or she has enough friends of the opposite sex; so he or she puts on good clothes, uses flower and fragrant scents (not a guarantee).

 

(f) Prapeditha: Troubled tormented. A planet is said to be in Prapeeditha, if it is defeated by another planet in the fight among themselves. Such a planet causes separation from close relatives and friends; he or she may wander from one place to the other (frequent transfers, keeping the family in any one place); suffer in the hands of the enemies or from ill-health. Thus he or she will be miserable, even a king will suffer (not always).

 

(g) "Dheena" Unhappy, timid worried, miserable. planet is said to be in Dheena conditions if either in Rasi Kundali or in Navamsa Kundali, it occupies its enemy's quarters; such a planet threatens loss of one's position, prestige and honour. The person is defeated. He is ruined. He goes to foreign places: cannot keep himself clean (not in all cases).

 

(h) "Kala" —— cruel —— wicked —— A planet is in 'kala' condition when it occupies either in Rasi kundali or Navamsa kundali, the sign belonging to any of the natural malefics, Sun, waning Moon, Mars, afflicted Mercury or Saturn. Such a planet always contributes for one's miserable life.

 

(i) "Vikala" —— unsteady, infirm, such a condition arises when a planet is eclipsed. (Saturn and Venus do not lose strength even when eclipsed. Uthrakalamirtha, kanda IV sloka —— 15), loss of wealth, separation from partner, mentally worried, enemy's strength much greater and enemy becoming more powerful, agony, etc., are threatened

 

(j) "Bheedhi", fear, threatening, frightened. A planet attains the condition of Bheedhi" in its Neecha, debilitated i.c., depression sign. Such a planet makes one diffident. There is no self-confidence. He is afraid by anything and everything. Loss of all kinds are portended.

Sloka 3: The astrologer should start his work only after bowing to the planets. He must worship, when truth will to dawn in his mind. Then commence to judge the query

 

The querist has to sit facing cast or north or north-east. He should avoid taking such a seat, facing west, south-west, north-west, south, and south-east directions.

 

Varahamihira in Brihat Samhita has advised the astrologer to take seat in an even ground under a tree with flowers or fruits; he has to avoid such trees where owls and such bad omen birds take rest; the name of the tree itself should be encouraging offering mental solace. Or one should prefer Deva, Rishi Twiya. Siddha or Sadhu Nilayam with Sath pushyam, i.e.. the temple, the Ashram of Maharishis. the residences of Brahmins, the places of Siddha Purushas or good Sadhus where there are planets with beautiful and sweet smelling flowers etc. Why? The sight itself should be pleasing to the eyes. Lawn is preferred.

 

Sloka 4: The consultant also should pray with fruits, flowers, gems and gold; then only, come out with his problems and put queries to the astrologer.

 

Avoid taking seat under a tree which is cut or which is thorny. Avoid graveyards, neglected temples, junction of the roads, dirty places, and dry barren ground. Reject such places where persons remain naked, or saloon, slaugher house gambling den, hospitals, prison or honey store room.

 

Why because one can have favourable results if the consultant faces east. If he faces south or north, journey is indicated. South-west and north-east show that there is long delay in achieving one's aim. North-west and south-east threaten failure, disappointment or temporary pleasure.

 

Regarding west, in Prasna Bairava it is taken as auspicious whereas Varahamihira advises to avoid west.

 

Both the astrologer and the consultant must prefer the morning hours to the afternoon the Santhyakala, twilight period and the nights.

 

Neelakanta suggests that the astrologer should check whether the consultant has approached him with the sincere idea of placing his problems and then requesting the astrologer to solve them or he has come to test him or put some questions casually. This can be judged by finding out whether the lagna that is rising in the east is occupied by Moon, squared or opposed by Saturn, or opposed by Mercury or aspected by Mars and Mercury in moudyam, i.e. eclipsed. If the ascendant, at the moment of query, has such afflictions, the consultant is not sincere. Benefics in the Lagna Bhava denotes that he is true and he has the real urge. Malefics in the lagna indicates that he is not plain and open. If lord of the 7th cusp receives aspects from its enemy or Jupiter or Mercury he is crooked and not straightforward. But if the 7th house as well as lagna receive aspects from either Moon or Mercury or Jupiter or by all of them he is honest when he puts the query. Benefics aspecting the enemy of lord of 7 when he aspects the latter, then also, he is honest.

 

Only when the consultant is serious and sincere, then only truth will come out; prediction will prove to be correct.

 

After satisfying himself, the astrologer has to note down the various questions, 1, 2, 3, and so on, if the person wants to consult more than one question. While judging the first question, take lagna as the first Bhava, for the second question find where Moon is deposited. Consider that sign as Lagna; for the third question, note the sign tenanted by Sun and take that sign as Lagna. For the fourth, the lagna should be the sign occupied by Jupiter; the fifth question is to be answered by taking either of the signs occupied by the stronger of the two planets Mercury or Venus and so on.

(Any difference in results between Natal and Horary chart.)

 

Sloka 1: In the previous six chapters how to predict the results for a person taking his birth horoscope is explained. If no horoscope is available the results can be predicted by taking the time of query. The results obtained by scrutinising the birth chart or the Horary chart erected for the time of query will be the same. They will not differ. If at the time of query it is cloudy and it is not possible to ascertain the exact time which would have passed from sunrise then by putting further questions to the querist, his horoscope can be erected.

 

(What the Consultant and Astrologer should do.)

 

Sloka 2: The consultant is advised to take fruits and fees to the astrologer. If the querist is asked to give any number between one and 108, he should give any one number. Then the astrologer takes the number for further calculation and prediction whether the result is favourable or not. The astrologer explains the past and future after he prays to his diety.

 

(How to erect the horoscope of a person who has lost it)

 

Sloka 3: Divide by 9 the number given by the consultant. Note down the quotient and the remainder. The former denotes how many rasis (signs) from Aries (Mesha) has to be counted and the latter indicates the exact Navamsa to be taken in the next sign. Work out the position of the planet and note down the Bhavas for this horoscope; one has to predict the results whether advantageous or not. One should consider the strength of the planets and also to which matters each planet is the Chief Governor.

 

Sloka 4: Calculate the number of flowers and the units of money given to the astrologer by the consultant. He is to multiply this figure by the number given by the consultant, and then divide by 12. Note down the remainder. Jupiter will be deposited in the horoscope of the consultant in that sign which is obtained by counting from Aries with the remainder. Jupiter at the time of consultation will be in a sign. The astrologer has to look at the consultant and Guess his age and calculate how many times Jupiter would have completed the cycle of the zodiac.

 

Note how far Jupiter has passed in the sign, from the commencement of that sign at the consultation. So much will be the number of months and days which would have passed from the previous birthday of the consultant. Thus by noting the number of cycles made by the Jupiter and also the proportionate movement of Jupiter in the sign at the time of consultation you calculate the time passed after his birth. Or the sum is to be deducted from the time of the query. The result is to be taken as the exact time of the birth of the consultant.

 

Sloka 5: All the above calculations ars in Solar System, i.e., the month, the day, the nazhikhai (ghatis!). (I Ghati = 24 minutes = 1 Nazhikhai). Having obtained the birth time one can calculate and erect the complete horoscope, of the consultant who would have lost his.

 

Sloka 6: The consultant has given a number; multiply it by 2 and then divide the product by three. The question will be about a living object if the remainder is one. If the remainder is two, it will be about some mineral; if the remainder is three or zero, then the question would be about a plant. If at the time of question the sign Aries is the Lagna it represents two- legged bipeds. If the lagna falls in Rishaba it represents four- legged quadrupeds. If the Ascendant be Gemini it denotes pregnancy. Legal dispute will be the matter if Cancer rises, Question will be about the Government if Leo is the lagna.

 

Sloka 7: If the Ascendant falls in Virgo, the question will be about the secret activities with a lady other than his own wife. If Libra is the ascendant the question relates to one's business. Son of Varahamihira
